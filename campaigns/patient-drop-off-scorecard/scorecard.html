How to Use This With Your Team

  1. Score independently. Have 3–5 stakeholders each complete the scorecard without comparing answers.
  2. Compare section scores. Where do people agree? Where do they disagree? Disagreements reveal blind spots.
  3. Identify the 2 weakest sections. These are your highest-ROI investment areas.
  4. Set a 90-day target. Pick 3–5 items within your weakest sections and define what a 1-point improvement looks like.
  5. Re-score in 90 days. Track progress. Use the scorecard as a recurring diagnostic.

Building the Business Case

The Problem

Patients enroll but don't start. Your program handles logistics well but doesn't address the psychological barriers — fear, doubt, lack of relatable support — that cause patients to stall.

The Cost

Every patient who enrolls but doesn't start represents lost therapy revenue, wasted acquisition spend, and reduced program ROI. For specialty therapies, a single patient's annual therapy value often exceeds $50,000.

The Solution

Peer-to-peer mentorship — connecting hesitant patients with trained mentors who've completed the same treatment. 68% more treatment starts. 29% better adherence. 133.5 more days on therapy.

If your program enrolls 1,000 patients per year and 40% don't start therapy, closing even half that gap with peer mentorship means 200 additional treatment starts. At $50,000 average annual therapy value, that's $10M in recovered revenue.
