# Twitter Thread: The Value Exchange Is Shifting

## Tweet 1: Hook

What do patients need from you that has nothing to do with your therapy?

Most pharma brands never ask.

Here's what changes when you do — and the data that proves it.

Thread 🧵

---

## Tweet 2

The old model: Offer access assistance, copay cards, reimbursement support. Patient stays on therapy. Transaction complete.

That era is over.

Reimbursement assistance is table stakes. Copay cards are expected. Every major brand has them.

---

## Tweet 3

Patients managing chronic conditions, rare diseases, or specialty therapies need far more than medication access.

They need:
• Connection to specialists
• Support for caregivers
• Community
• Someone who's been there

---

## Tweet 4

A rare disease brand asked: What makes living with this disease difficult beyond the medication itself?

Answers had nothing to do with their therapy. Specialist access. Workplace discrimination. Isolation. Caregiver burnout.

They built a program for all of it. Including peer matching.

---

## Tweet 5

Results:
• 40% higher enrollment than projected
• Patients stayed engaged long after typical touchpoints
• They referred other patients
• Became brand advocates

Patient feedback: "This is the first pharma company that treated me like a whole person, not just a prescription to fill."

---

## Tweet 6

The data on peer-to-peer mentorship:

• 68% more likely to begin treatment
• 71% say mentorship was critical to their decision
• 73% connection rate (vs. 10–20% traditional)
• 90% confidence boost
• 133.5 more days on therapy

---

## Tweet 7

Fear, doubt, and lack of relatable support drive most drop-offs. Not prior auth. Not copay.

Hubs solve operational barriers. The psychological barriers are the missing middle.

Peer-to-peer is the operational solution.
