# LinkedIn Text Post 02: The Missing Middle

Most patient drop-offs don't happen because of prior auth or copay.

They happen because of fear. Doubt. Lack of relatable support.

Hubs solve the operational barriers. They're good at it. Prior auth, reimbursement, specialty pharmacy coordination—those problems have solutions.

The psychological barriers are the missing middle. And most patient support programs don't touch them.

Patients don't need more information. They need someone who gets it.

Peer-to-peer mentorship is the operational solution. Connect patients to people who've walked the same path. Real conversations. Real time. Not brochures. Not chatbots. Not call centers.

The data backs it up:

→ 68% more likely to begin prescribed treatment
→ 71% report mentorship as critical to their decision
→ 73% connection rate (vs. 10–20% for traditional programs)
→ 90% confidence boost
→ 133.5 more days on therapy

When you support patients as whole people, they engage more deeply. They stay on therapy longer. They become advocates.

The expanded value exchange drives both better patient outcomes and better commercial outcomes.

Peer-to-peer isn't a nice-to-have. It's the backbone.
