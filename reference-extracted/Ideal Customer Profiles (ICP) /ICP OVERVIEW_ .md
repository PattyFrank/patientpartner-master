## **Commercialized Drugs**

**Company Types:**


  - **Large Pharmaceutical Companies:** These companies typically have a large product

portfolio consisting of 10+ commercialized drugs (think of companies like Pfizer,
Novartis, Bayer, Eli Lilly, etc…)


  - **Small Pharmaceutical Companies** : These companies typically only have 1 maybe 2

FDA approved drugs on the market.


**Focus Areas:**


  - **Branded Drugs:** All the money is focused on branded drugs that still maintain some

kind of exclusivity or patent protection. Simply put: branded drugs= $$$ marketing
budget……. Generic drugs= No marketing budget.

  - **Therapy Areas** : We are seeing most success in areas like

     - Oncology

     - Rare Disease

     - Chronic Conditions

    - Neuro

     - Dermatology

     - etc.

  - **New Drug Launches:** Anytime a new drug gets approved by FDA the manufacturer will

allocate a MASSIVE budget to the launch of that product. Particularly those that are
entering competitive markets and need to ensure high levels of patient adherence and
engagement from the outset.

  - **Specialty Medications:** These medications often require specialized support, which

makes patient engagement and adherence critical for success. Additionally they tend to
be the most expensive, making the ROI of our program easily obtainable.

**Pain Points:**

The PatientPartner technology is a solution for the 2 largest challenges for ANY branded drug
on the market. Those challenges are either:
1) “We need more patients to start taking our drug”


   - Also known as


        - New Patient Starts (This is the most recognized term to use)

        - Script Lift

        - Patient Conversion

        - Patient Activiation

2) “We need to make sure patients stay on our drug”.


   - Also known as


        - Patient Adherence (This is the most recognized term to use)

        - Patient Persistency

        - Patient Retention


  - **Adherence Challenges:** Many patients struggle to adhere to their prescribed

medication regimens, leading to suboptimal outcomes and increased healthcare costs.

  - **Patient Engagement Gaps:** Traditional engagement methods may fail to resonate with

patients, leading to disengagement and reduced treatment success.

  - **Data Collection Needs:** Pharmaceutical companies increasingly need real-world

evidence (RWE) and patient-reported outcomes (PROs) to demonstrate the

effectiveness and value of their treatments.


**Why They Need PatientPartner:**


  - **Growing New Patient Starts:** Help patients who are diagnosed with the disease but not

on drug, by connecting them with a relatable mentor who is currently taking that drug.
That conversation leads to more patients starting the drug for the first time.


  - **Improved Adherence:** By connecting patients with mentors who have successfully

navigated similar treatments, PatientPartner enhances adherence and reduces dropout

rates.

  - **Enhanced Patient Experience:** The mentor relationship offers emotional and practical

support, leading to higher patient satisfaction and better overall outcomes.


  - **Valuable Insights:** The platform gathers actionable insights into patient experiences,

which can be used to refine support programs, improve engagement strategies, and

bolster clinical and commercial success.

### **2. Enterprise B2B Med-Tech / Med-Device**


**Target Roles:**


  - **VP/Director of Patient Experience:** Tasked with ensuring that patients have a

seamless and positive experience with the company’s devices, from initial use to long
term adherence.

  - **VP/Director of Sales & Marketing:** Responsible for driving the adoption of medical

devices through targeted marketing strategies and ensuring that patients understand the
benefits and usage of the devices.

  - **VP/Director of Clinical Operations:** Focuses on the operational aspects of deploying

devices in clinical settings, ensuring that patients and healthcare providers are wellsupported.

  - **Head of Product Development:** Oversees the development of new devices, with a

focus on user-friendliness and patient-centered design.

  - **Chief Technology Officer:** Leads the technological innovations within the company,

ensuring that devices are not only effective but also enhance patient outcomes.


**Company Types:**


  - **Medical Device Manufacturers:** Companies developing devices that require patient

education and ongoing support for optimal use.

  - **Diagnostic Tool Developers:** Firms producing diagnostic tools that need to be

seamlessly integrated into patients’ lives to ensure consistent use and accurate results.

  - **Health Tech Solution Providers:** Companies offering digital health solutions that

require patient engagement to demonstrate value and efficacy.


**Pain Points:**


  - **Correct Device Usage:** Many patients struggle to use medical devices correctly, leading

to poor outcomes and frustration.

  - **Device Abandonment:** Without proper support, patients may abandon their devices,

leading to wasted resources and unmet healthcare needs.

  - **Lack of Real-World Data:** Med-tech companies often lack detailed insights into how

their devices are used in everyday settings, limiting their ability to make data-driven
improvements.


**Why They Need PatientPartner:**


  - **Support for Correct Usage:** PatientPartner provides patients with mentors who can

guide them in using their devices correctly, reducing abandonment rates and improving

outcomes.

  - **Continuous Engagement:** The platform ensures that patients remain engaged with

their devices, leading to better long-term adherence and satisfaction.

  - **Actionable Data:** By capturing real-world data on device usage, PatientPartner helps

companies refine their products and better understand patient needs.
