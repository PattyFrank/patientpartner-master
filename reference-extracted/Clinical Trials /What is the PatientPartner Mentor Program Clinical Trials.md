## What Is the Site’s Role?

###### Introduction & Education

Site staff introduce the Mentor Program, explain its purpose and
voluntary nature, and provide the participant with the official program

materials and access link.


Site staff may assist participants with basic access questions but do not

provide technical support beyond basic troubleshooting.


Participation in the Mentor Program is voluntary and does **not** affect

trial care in any way.

# SUPPORT FOR CLINICAL

## **TRIAL PARTICIPANTS CAN FIND** **SUPPORT FROM OTHERS WHO HAVE** **“BEEN IN THEIR SHOES”**

The PatientPartner Mentor Program pairs trial
participants with experienced peers who offer relatable
support, helping them feel informed, confident, and
motivated throughout the study.








#### **This Program Is Made Possible By**


### What Does A Mentor Do? Mentors share their personal journey with their condition and clinical Mentor Program to Trial Participants

trial participation in a way that provides perspective and

understanding to others. Their role is to listen, share their
experience, and help others feel supported as they navigate their trial

journey. Mentors support trial participants by:


Offering emotional support and understanding
Sharing personal experience living with their disease and diagnosis
Describing what it was like to participate in a clinical trial, from their

### How Will Sites Work With PatientPartner?

PatientPartner will work closely with study sites to ensure clear
understanding of the program, defined escalation pathways, and alignment

on key participant touchpoints. Below are several ways we partner with sites

throughout implementation and ongoing execution.


Training on the program and tips for introducing it to participants
Providing feedback and analysis of patient sentiment, common trends
related to patient satisfaction, motivation and concerns

Escalation activities related to drop-out, safety or compliance risks


#### How Participants Engage With a Mentor

1. **Introduction at Site Visit:**


Eligible participants are introduced to the Mentor Program during a

study visit. Participation is voluntary.

2. **Secure Enrollment**


Interested participants access a secure link to provide consent and

complete a short matching questionnaire.
3. **Relatable Matching**

Participants are paired with a trained mentor using PatientPartner’s
proprietary matching algorithm based on clinical, behavioral, and
personal factors.

4. **Ongoing Connection**

Once matched, mentors and participants can chat at any point

throughout the trial.

5. **Secure Communication and Escalation**


All communications occur within a secure platform. Any concerns (AEs,

withdrawal risk, etc) are escalated per sponsor and site procedures.

#### How Do Trial Participants Engage With a Mentor?

Participation in the Mentor Program is completely voluntary
Eligible participants are introduced to the program during a site visit

Interested participants access a secure PatientPartner link to:


Provide consent

Complete a brief questionnaire


Using PatientPartner’s proprietary matching algorithm, participants are
paired with a mentor based on relevant psychological, behavioral, and

clinical factors.


Once matched, the mentor will reach out to begin the connection.
The participant controls how often they engage.


All communication happens within the secure platform, and any

concerns (such as adverse events or risk of withdrawal) ar escalated

according to Sponsor and Site procedures.
